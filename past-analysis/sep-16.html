Here are some popular Bank Nifty options strategies that traders often use:

Straddle

What It Is: Buying both a call and a put option at the same strike price and expiration date.
When to Use: When you expect high volatility but are unsure of the direction.

Strangle

What It Is: Buying a call option and a put option with different strike prices but the same expiration.
When to Use: Similar to a straddle, for anticipated volatility, but with a potentially lower cost.

Iron Condor

What It Is: Selling an out-of-the-money call and put while simultaneously buying further out-of-the-money options to limit risk.
When to Use: When you expect low volatility and the Bank Nifty to stay within a certain range.

Bull Call Spread

What It Is: Buying a call option at a lower strike price and selling another call option at a higher strike price.
When to Use: When you expect moderate upward movement in Bank Nifty.

Bear Put Spread

What It Is: Buying a put option at a higher strike price and selling another put option at a lower strike price.
When to Use: When you expect moderate downward movement.

Calendar Spread

What It Is: Buying a longer-dated option and selling a shorter-dated option at the same strike price.
When to Use: When you expect low volatility in the near term but anticipate movement in the longer term.

Covered Call

What It Is: Holding a long position in Bank Nifty and selling call options against that position.
When to Use: To generate income in a sideways market while being willing to sell your position if the stock rises.

Protective Put

What It Is: Buying a put option while holding a long position in Bank Nifty.
When to Use: To protect against downside risk.
